Introduction to the European Lion

The European Lion (Panthera leo europaea) once roamed vast areas of southern Europe during the Pleistocene era. It is believed to be a close relative of modern African lions, though it eventually went extinct due to changing climates and competition with humans. The European Lion featured prominently in Greek and Roman mythology, symbolizing strength and courage in many ancient cultures.

Habitat and Distribution

Fossil evidence suggests that the European Lion lived in regions stretching from modern-day Spain to Italy, as well as parts of Greece. The lion adapted to a variety of environments, including forests and plains. Over time, human expansion and climate shifts led to the lion's gradual disappearance from Europe, with the last individuals believed to have lived in Greece.
